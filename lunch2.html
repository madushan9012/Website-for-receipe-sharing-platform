Seafood Laksa

Instructions:

Prepare the Laksa paste:
Heat the oil in a large frying pan, and add the chopped onions. Cook on medium for about 5 minutes until the onions soften and start to turn translucent.

Ingredients for Laksa Paste:
3 tbsp oil, 1 onion, 4 red chillies, 2 tsp ginger paste, 2 cloves garlic, 1 tsp lemongrass paste, 1 heaped tbsp fresh coriander stalks, 1.5 tsp turmeric, ½ tsp cumin, ½ tsp paprika, 1 tsp tamarind paste.

Add in the rest of the Laksa paste ingredients, stir and continue to cook for 5 minutes, stirring occasionally.

While the laksa paste ingredients are frying, make the garnish. To a small bowl, add the vinegar, sugar, chilli, cucumber, red onion, and coriander. Stir together, then put to one side.
Ingredients for Garnish:
1 tsp rice wine vinegar, 1 tsp caster sugar, 1 red chilli, 1 finger-sized piece of cucumber, ¼ small red onion, small handful of chopped coriander (cilantro).

Turn off the heat from the frying pan and transfer the onions-chilli-spice mixture to a mini chopper or food processor. Blend together until it forms a paste. Add the laksa paste back into the frying pan.

Turn the heat to medium-high and add the coconut milk, fish sauce, and stock. Stir together and heat through until just simmering.

Ingredients for Soup:
200 ml full-fat coconut milk, 1 tsp fish sauce, 200 ml chicken or seafood stock.

Add the fish, stir, and allow to cook for 4 minutes, turning a couple of times, until the fish is starting to fall apart.

Ingredients for Seafood:
2 cod or haddock fillets.

While the fish is cooking, soak the noodles in boiling water until soft (about 3 minutes), then drain.

Ingredients for Noodles:
200 g dried rice/vermicelli noodles.

Add in the prawns, stir, and cook for one minute. The prawns should be starting to turn pink, and your fish should be cooked.

Ingredients for Seafood:
12-16 king prawns.

Finally, add in the beansprouts and cook for another minute, so they're warmed through but still slightly crisp. Turn off the heat.

Ingredients for Soup:
200 g beansprouts.

Divide the drained noodles between bowls and spoon the laksa sauce and seafood over the top.
Top each bowl with a spoonful of the garnish you made earlier, and serve.

Notes:

Can I make Chicken Laksa instead?
Yes, you can replace the haddock and prawns with cooked shredded chicken. Alternatively, add pieces of raw chicken in with the blended paste and cook until the chicken is sealed. Then add the coconut milk and stock and simmer until the chicken is cooked through.

Can I freeze Laksa?
The noodles, seafood, and beansprouts won't freeze very well. If you want to get ahead, I suggest making the paste and freezing it in portions. Defrost and continue with the recipe from step 4 (make the garnish on the day of serving).

Can I make low-fat Laksa?
Use low-fat coconut milk, although it has more of a tendency to split. To prevent this, add the low-fat coconut milk at the end of cooking and heat through until hot, but avoid boiling it to prevent splitting.

Can I make ahead for lunch the next day?
Prepare the soup up to the point just before adding the beansprouts, cool, and refrigerate. Reheat the sauce for a couple of minutes, throw in the beansprouts for one final minute, and pour over your pre-cooked noodles.
