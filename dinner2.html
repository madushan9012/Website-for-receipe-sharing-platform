Chicken Schnitzel with Garlic-Caper Butter

Instructions:

Butterfly the chicken breasts by slicing into each breast horizontally most of the way through, then opening up the chicken breast – so it looks like an extra-large chicken steak.
Ingredients:
4 chicken breasts

Place one of the chicken breasts on top of a large piece of baking parchment and fold the parchment over the top of the chicken. Use a rolling pin or wooden mallet to pound the chicken breast until it’s about ¼ inch (just over half a centimetre) thick. Repeat with the remaining chicken breasts.

Mix together the flour, salt, pepper, garlic powder, and paprika in a large shallow bowl.
60 g (1/2 cup) plain (all-purpose) flour, 1/2 tsp salt, 1/2 tsp black pepper, 1 tsp garlic powder, 1 tsp paprika

Break the eggs in a second large shallow bowl and whisk lightly with a fork.
2 large eggs

Add the panko breadcrumbs to a third large shallow bowl.
115 g (2 + 1/4 cups) panko breadcrumbs

Dredge a chicken breast through the flour mixture, coating fully on both sides. Coat fully with the egg mixture, then finally coat well with the panko mixture. Repeat with the remaining chicken breasts.

Place a baking tray in the oven on a low temperature – this is to keep the cooked schnitzels warm. We’ll be cooking in two batches.

Add the oil to a large frying pan and heat over medium heat.
240 ml (1 cup) oil

Add two of the chicken breasts and cook for 2-3 minutes on each side until golden and cooked through (check it's no longer pink in the middle). Carefully remove from the pan and place on the tray in the oven.

Repeat with the remaining two chicken breasts. You shouldn’t need to add more oil to the pan, but you can add an extra splash if needed. Make sure the oil is hot before adding the chicken.

Garlic-Caper Butter: Place the butter in a saucepan and heat over low-medium heat until the butter melts, starts to foam a little, and turns very lightly golden brown (about 3-4 minutes).
120 g (1/2 cup) unsalted butter

Turn the heat down to low and add the garlic. Cook for a further minute, stirring, to gently sauté the garlic.
3 cloves garlic

Turn off the heat, then stir in the salt, pepper, capers, and parsley.
1/2 tsp salt, 1/2 tsp black pepper, 2 tbsp capers in brine, 3 tbsp finely chopped fresh parsley

Divide the chicken between plates and drizzle with the garlic-caper butter. Serve with lemon wedges, steamed new potatoes, and a butter lettuce salad.
wedges of lemon, steamed new potatoes, Butter Lettuce Salad

Notes:

Can I cook in the air fryer or oven? I don’t recommend cooking this in the air fryer or oven, as the chicken is thin and will cook before the coating has a chance to brown.

Leftovers/make-ahead: You can make the crispy chicken and fry it ahead of time, then cool, cover, and refrigerate for up to 2 days. Reheat in the oven at 180C/350F (fan) for 8-10 minutes until piping hot throughout. Alternatively, reheat in the air fryer at 180C/350F for 4-6 minutes per schnitzel.

Freezing: Make the crispy chicken and fry it, then cool, cover, and freeze in a single layer. Defrost overnight in the fridge and reheat as per instructions above.
